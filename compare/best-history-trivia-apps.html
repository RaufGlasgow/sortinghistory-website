1. Sorting History

Best for: History enthusiasts who want to actually learn timelines, not just answer A/B/C/D.

What makes it different

Sorting History is the only app on this list where you arrange events in chronological order instead of answering multiple choice questions. You see historical events, you drag them into the correct timeline. It's a fundamentally different game mechanic that tests (and teaches) your understanding of how history fits together.

Strengths

  • Deep history content: 12 dedicated categories at launch (US History, World Wars, Ancient Civilizations, European History, Scientific Discoveries, and more). 100+ researched events per category. 1,200+ events total.
  • You learn even when you're wrong: After each round, you see the full correct timeline with dates and context. Every game is a mini history lesson.
  • No timer: Take your time. Think it through. History rewards knowledge, not speed.
  • Two game modes: Sorting History (arrange events in order) and History Pinpoint (guess the exact year).
  • Three difficulty levels: Friendly (no penalties), Competitive, Expert.
  • Local multiplayer: Pass & Play (2-6 teams, one device) and Network Play (two devices, same WiFi).
  • Works offline: Everything is stored locally. Play on a plane.
  • 4 languages: English, German, Portuguese, Dutch.
  • Community votes on new content: Two new categories added monthly. You vote on what's next.

Limitations

  • History only. If you want science, sports, or pop culture trivia, look elsewhere.
  • No online matchmaking. Multiplayer is local only.
  • 4 languages (not 25 like Trivia Crack).
  • New app. Smaller community than established games.

Price: Free (8 categories, 800+ events). Explorer: $1.99/mo or $9.99/yr. Historian: $2.99/mo or $14.99/yr. Lifetime options available.

2. Trivia Crack

Best for: Playing trivia against friends or strangers online across many topics.

Strengths

  • Massive community: Hundreds of millions of downloads. Easy to find opponents.
  • Topic variety: Science, Entertainment, Art, Geography, Sports, and History. Something for everyone.
  • Online multiplayer: Challenge friends or random opponents worldwide.
  • 25 languages.
  • User-created content: AI-powered quiz creator lets anyone add questions.

Limitations

  • History is just one of six categories. You'll spend most of your time on other topics.
  • Multiple choice format. You can guess without knowing anything.
  • Timed answers. No time to think deeply.
  • Heavy ads and in-app purchase prompts in the free tier.
  • User-created content quality varies significantly.

Price: Free with ads and in-app purchases ($0.99-$24.99).

3. History: Quiz Game & Trivia

Best for: Quick, casual history quizzes organized by era.

Strengths

  • History-focused: 600 questions across 4 eras (Prehistory, Ancient, Medieval, Modern).
  • Simple and clean. No complicated features. Just quiz questions.
  • Free.

Limitations

  • 600 questions runs out faster than you'd expect.
  • Standard multiple choice. Same format as every other quiz app.
  • 4 broad eras, not specific category topics.
  • Limited replay value once you've seen most questions.

Price: Free with ads.

4. World History Trivia Quiz

Best for: A quick 15-minute history quiz session.

Strengths

  • Completely free. No subscriptions, no paywalls.
  • Simple. 150 questions, pick your answer, done.

Limitations

  • 150 questions total. You'll exhaust the content quickly.
  • No categories. General history only.
  • No multiplayer.
  • No new content added.

Price: Free.

5. Timeline (Board Game by Asmodee)

Best for: Screen-free game nights with a physical timeline mechanic.

Strengths

  • Tactile experience. Physical cards feel good in your hands.
  • No screens. Great for families who want a break from devices.
  • Gift-worthy. A nicely packaged box beats an app download as a present.
  • Same core mechanic as Sorting History: placing events in chronological order.

Limitations

  • ~100 events per box. Content runs out.
  • New content means buying more boxes ($15-20 each).
  • Cards wear out, bend, and show date imprints.
  • Requires in-person play. No solo or remote option.
  • No difficulty options.

Price: $15-20 per box.
