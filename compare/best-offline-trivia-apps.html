What makes it different

Sorting History stores everything on your device. All 12 categories, all 1,200+ historical events, every game mode. You can switch your phone to airplane mode and play for hours without hitting a single loading screen. This is not a "limited offline mode" — it is the full game, offline by default.

3. QuizUp (Shut Down)

Best for: No one, anymore. QuizUp shut down permanently in 2023.

What happened

QuizUp was one of the most popular trivia apps ever made. At its peak it had millions of active players, hundreds of topic categories, and a vibrant community. Then the servers closed. Because QuizUp was entirely online — no offline mode, no local data storage — everything vanished. Player progress, scores, custom quizzes, community content. All gone.

Why it matters for this list

  • Online-only trivia apps are a risk. If the company shuts down, your game disappears.
  • No offline fallback meant total loss. Players could not even access their favorite quizzes after the shutdown.
  • Apps that store content locally survive server shutdowns. Your data stays on your device regardless of what happens to the company's servers.

QuizUp is included here as a reminder: when choosing a trivia app, consider what happens if the servers go away. Apps with full offline support keep working no matter what.

Price: No longer available.

4. Trivia Star

Best for: Casual players who want quick general knowledge quizzes they can play anywhere.

Strengths

  • Works offline: Questions are stored locally and can be played without an internet connection.
  • General knowledge coverage: Covers a wide range of topics from science to history to pop culture.
  • Simple format: Multiple choice questions with increasing difficulty. Easy to pick up.
  • Free to play.

Limitations

  • General knowledge only. No deep category specialization.
  • Ad-supported. Frequent ads between rounds.
  • Multiple choice format only. No variety in question types.
  • Content can feel repetitive over time.

Price: Free with ads. In-app purchases to remove ads.

5. 94%

Best for: Casual puzzle fans who want an offline word and association game.

Strengths

  • Works offline: Levels are stored locally and playable without internet.
  • Unique format: Instead of answering questions, you guess what 94% of people said about a topic (similar to Family Feud). A refreshing change from standard trivia.
  • Hundreds of levels. Keeps you busy for a while.
  • Free to start.

Limitations

  • Not traditional trivia. This is an association and guessing game, not a knowledge quiz.
  • Once you finish all levels, there is limited replay value.
  • Ad-supported. Ads appear between levels.
  • Not a history game. Content is general pop culture and common knowledge.

Price: Free with ads. In-app purchases for hints and ad removal.
