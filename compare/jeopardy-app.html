Looking for a Trivia Game That Goes Deeper Than Jeopardy?

The Jeopardy app is great for general knowledge. But if you love history and want more than surface-level questions, Sorting History might be what you're looking for. Here's how the two compare.

What Is the Jeopardy App?

The Jeopardy! app is the official mobile game from Sony Pictures Television, based on the iconic TV game show. It features the classic Jeopardy format: categories, Daily Doubles, Final Jeopardy, and the answer-in-the-form-of-a-question mechanic. Players compete against other people online through matchmaking.

It's free to download with ads and in-app purchases, available on both iOS and Android, and has millions of downloads. Jeopardy is a cultural institution. We respect it enormously.

What Do We Love About the Jeopardy App?

Jeopardy is a beloved institution for good reason:

  • The format is iconic. Answer-in-the-form-of-a-question has been a part of pop culture for decades. Nothing else feels quite like it.
  • Massive topic range. From Shakespeare to science to sports to pop culture. If you want variety, Jeopardy delivers it.
  • Competitive energy. Online matchmaking means there's always someone to play against, and the buzzer mechanic creates real tension.
  • Brand recognition. Everyone knows what Jeopardy is. It's the gold standard of trivia.

If you want a fast-paced general knowledge game with competitive online play, the Jeopardy app does that well.

Where Sorting History Is Different

Sorting History isn't trying to be Jeopardy. It's a different kind of trivia experience for people who want to go deep on history.

"I want to learn, not just recall"

Jeopardy tests what you already know. You hear a clue, you buzz in, you either know it or you don't. Sorting History works differently. You see historical events and arrange them in chronological order. Even when you get the order wrong, you see the full correct timeline with dates and context. Every round teaches you something new about how events connect across time.

"History gets lost in the shuffle"

In Jeopardy, history is one of many rotating categories. You might get a history board, or you might not. Sorting History is 100% history, all the time. Twelve dedicated categories at launch — US History, World Wars, Ancient Civilizations, Scientific Discoveries, European History, Sports History, and more — each with 100+ researched events.

"I don't want to race a buzzer"

Jeopardy rewards speed. The person who buzzes in first wins, even if others knew the answer. Sorting History has no timer. You take as long as you need to think through the timeline. Was the printing press before or after the fall of Constantinople? Take your time. History rewards thinking, not reflexes.

"I want to play without internet"

The Jeopardy app requires an internet connection for online matchmaking and most features. Sorting History works completely offline. All 1,200+ events are stored on your device. Play on a flight, on a road trip, or anywhere without cell service.

"I want a quick daily habit"

Sorting History has a Daily Challenge — a quick 4-event sorting puzzle in a surprise category, every single day. It takes less than 5 minutes. Build a streak and make it part of your morning routine.

Where Does Jeopardy Win?

We're honest about where the Jeopardy app has clear advantages:

  • Topic variety. Jeopardy covers every subject imaginable. If you want science, literature, pop culture, geography, and sports alongside history, Jeopardy has it all.
  • Online multiplayer. Compete against random opponents worldwide. Sorting History multiplayer is local only (WiFi and Pass & Play).
  • The Jeopardy brand. There's something special about playing the actual Jeopardy format with the real categories, Daily Doubles, and Final Jeopardy. No other app can replicate that feeling.
  • Cross-platform. Jeopardy is on both iOS and Android. Sorting History is currently iOS only.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Jeopardy if:

  • You want general knowledge trivia across many subjects
  • You enjoy the competitive buzzer format and racing to answer first
  • You want to play against random opponents online
  • You love the Jeopardy TV show format specifically
  • You use an Android device

Choose Sorting History if:

  • History is your thing and you want serious depth (12 categories, 100+ events each)
  • You want to learn something new, not just test what you already know
  • You prefer thinking through a problem without a timer
  • You want to play offline — flights, commutes, anywhere
  • You want local multiplayer for family game night (2-6 teams)
  • You want difficulty options (Friendly for learning, Expert for serious history buffs)
  • You want a Daily Challenge to build a daily habit

Or play both

They serve different needs. Use Jeopardy for fast-paced general trivia against online opponents. Use Sorting History when you want to slow down and go deep on history at your own pace. Both have free tiers, so there's nothing to lose by trying.
