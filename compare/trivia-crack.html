Sorting History vs Trivia Crack

Trivia Crack is one of the most popular trivia apps ever made. But if you love history specifically, here's why Sorting History might be what you're looking for.

What Is Trivia Crack?

Trivia Crack is a general-knowledge trivia game with hundreds of millions of downloads worldwide. It covers six broad categories: Science, Entertainment, Art, Geography, Sports, and History. Players answer multiple-choice questions, spin a wheel, and compete against friends or strangers online.

It's a great game for general trivia. But if history is your thing, it only scratches the surface.

They're Different Games for Different People

This isn't really a "which is better" comparison. Trivia Crack and Sorting History do fundamentally different things.

Trivia Crack is a mile wide

It covers everything: science, sports, entertainment, geography, art, and history. If you want variety across topics and enjoy competing with random opponents online, Trivia Crack is excellent at that. It's a social game first, a learning tool second.

Sorting History is a mile deep

It does one thing: history. But it does it with 12 dedicated categories, 100+ researched events per category, and a game mechanic that teaches you the actual timeline. You don't pick A/B/C/D and hope for the best. You arrange events in order. You have to actually think about whether the printing press came before or after the fall of Constantinople.

Why History Fans Switch to Sorting History

"Multiple choice doesn't teach me anything"

In Trivia Crack, you pick from four options. If you don't know, you guess. If you guess right, you move on having learned nothing. In Sorting History, you build a timeline. Even when you're wrong, you see the correct order with dates and context. Every round is a mini history lesson.

"History is just one of six categories"

In Trivia Crack, you might get 2-3 history questions in a game. The rest is sports, entertainment, and geography. Sorting History is 100% history, all the time. US History, World Wars, Ancient Civilizations, Scientific Discoveries, European History, and more.

"I don't want a timer"

Trivia Crack's timed format rewards quick recall. Sorting History has no timer at all. Think about it. Look at the events. Reason through the timeline. History rewards knowledge, not speed.

"I want to play offline"

Trivia Crack needs an internet connection for most features. Sorting History works completely offline. All your categories and events are stored locally on your device. Play on a plane, on a train, or anywhere without signal.

Where Trivia Crack Wins

We're transparent about where Trivia Crack has the advantage:

  • Topic variety. If you want science, sports, entertainment, geography, and art alongside history, Trivia Crack covers it all.
  • Online matchmaking. Play against random opponents worldwide. Sorting History multiplayer is local (WiFi and Pass & Play).
  • Massive community. Hundreds of millions of players. User-created content. Social features.
  • More languages. Trivia Crack supports 25 languages. Sorting History currently supports 4.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Trivia Crack if:

  • You want variety across many topics, not just history
  • You want to compete against random opponents online
  • You prefer quick-fire multiple choice questions
  • You want a huge global community

Choose Sorting History if:

  • History is your thing and you want serious depth
  • You want to actually learn, not just guess A/B/C/D
  • You prefer to think without a timer
  • You want to play offline on flights, commutes, or travels
  • You want local multiplayer for family game night (2-6 teams)
  • You want difficulty options (Friendly for learning, Expert for challenge)

Or choose both

They complement each other. Play Trivia Crack for general knowledge with friends online. Play Sorting History when you want to go deep on history at your own pace. Both have free tiers, so there's no risk in trying.
