Frequently Asked Questions

What is Sorting History?

Sorting History is a history trivia game for iPhone and iPad where you sort historical events in chronological order. It features 12 categories at launch, growing monthly covering everything from ancient civilizations to modern pop culture, with solo and multiplayer modes and customizable game lengths from 5 to 30 rounds.

Can I play alone or do I need other people?

Both! Solo Adventure mode lets you play alone and earn ranks based on your accuracy. Pass & Play lets teams take turns on one device, and Network Play connects multiple devices over local WiFi for group competitions.

Is Sorting History free?

Yes ‚Äî Sorting History is free to download and play with 8 categories. Free players see ads between games but can unlock any category for 1 hour by watching an ad. Subscribe to Explorer or Historian for all 12 categories ad-free. Subscriptions are available monthly, annually, or as a one-time lifetime purchase.

What do I get with Explorer or Historian?

Both Explorer and Historian give you all 12 categories ad-free, with new categories on release day. Historian adds expansion packs (deep-dive content with 100+ extra events per category), the History Pinpoint bonus game mode, and an exclusive vote on which expansion pack gets built next. All paid tiers are available as monthly or annual subscriptions, or a one-time lifetime purchase.

What devices does Sorting History work on?

Sorting History runs on iPhone and iPad with iOS 15.0 or later.

Is this game good for families with kids?

Absolutely! Sorting History is designed to be family-friendly and educational. It's a great way for kids to learn history while having fun with parents and siblings. The Friendly scoring mode removes penalties for wrong answers, making it perfect for younger players.

Can teachers use this in classrooms?

Yes! Network Play connects multiple devices over local WiFi for classroom competitions. Teachers can select specific categories that align with their curriculum and adjust game length to fit class periods. Network Play is available to all players, including free accounts.

Does Network Play require a subscription?

No. Network Play over local WiFi is available to all players regardless of tier. You just need two or more devices on the same WiFi network.
